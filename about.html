Discover the Legacy of Louis Vuitton

From humble beginnings to a global luxury icon, explore the story of timeless craftsmanship.

Our Story Through Time

1837

Louis Vuitton Arrives in Paris

At 16 years old, Louis Vuitton arrived in Paris eager to learn a trade. He soon began an apprenticeship with Monsieur Maréchal, a skilled trunk maker and packer for wealthy travelers. There, Louis mastered the art of creating bespoke trunks, learning how to combine durability, precision, and elegance to protect valuables during long journeys. This early experience shaped his understanding of craftsmanship and laid the foundation for what would later become the Louis Vuitton legacy.

1854

Establishing the Brand

In 1854, Louis Vuitton opened his first workshop on Rue Neuve des Capucines in Paris, marking the official beginning of the Louis Vuitton brand. Drawing on years of craftsmanship, he introduced innovative flat-topped trunks that were lighter, more durable, and easily stackable—transforming the way people traveled and setting new standards for modern luggage..

1896

Introduction of the Monogram Canvas

The iconic LV Monogram canvas was introduced to protect Louis Vuitton designs from counterfeiting. Inspired by Japanese motifs and Victorian-era decorative patterns, the monogram became both a practical solution and a lasting symbol of the brand’s identity and craftsmanship.

1930s

Iconic Bags Are Born

Louis Vuitton introduced the Keepall, Noé, and Speedy bags, each designed to meet practical travel and lifestyle needs. Over time, these designs became timeless icons, celebrated for their functionality, craftsmanship, and enduring luxury.

Today

A Global Luxury Icon

Today, Louis Vuitton stands as a symbol of global luxury, seamlessly blending heritage craftsmanship with modern innovation. Its products from luggage to fashion and accessories are admired and coveted around the world, reflecting a legacy of quality, style, and timeless elegance.
